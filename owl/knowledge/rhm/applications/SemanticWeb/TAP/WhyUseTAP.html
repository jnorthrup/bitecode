Why use the Stanford TAP KB?

Why use the Stanford TAP KB?

Paraphrasing the Stanford TAP KB philosophy :

  • the KB contains a little bit of knowledge
    about many popular things;
  • the KB complements systems such as Cyc which have
    a lot of common-sense knowledge about many everyday things;
  • the KB complements "Upper Ontology" systems which have
    a lot of knowledge about a few abstract things.

In practical terms, this means that:

  • The KB can provide answers to a lot of simple questions.
    For example:
    • Who plays for the Boston Celtics?
    • Where is Carleton College?
  • When the KB doesn't have the answers, it may provide details
    (for example: amazonMap, dmozMap, ebayMap, looksmartMap)
    which facilitate finding the answer with systems such as
    Google, Amazon.com, TicketMaster, etc.
    For example:
    • Who is the CEO of Microsoft?
    • What is the average cost of a meal at Sardi's?
    • Where can I get Panofsky and Phillips' textbook?
    • When does Los Angeles play Sacramento?
  • The KB will not be able to answer questions which require
    common-sense reasoning; OpenCyc will have to be used.
    For example:
    • How do I get from my house to the airport?
    • Why did the chicken cross the road?
