Prototypes and Invariants

Prototypes and Invariants

Basic Ideas

  • When people think of concepts, they think of prototypes. But the invariants of a prototype are not the invariants of a concept.
  • Matching a prototype is a sufficient, but not necessary, condition for a unit to be subsumed under a concept.

Examples

bird prototype
X is a typical bird =
X can fly
X has feathers
...
bird definition
X is a kind of bird =
when people think of X, they think of a typical bird
some X can't fly
some X don't have feathers
...
